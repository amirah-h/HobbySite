Mary Stuart

10 facts about Mary Stuart

  • Mary Queen of Scots was born on 8th December 1542 and was executed on 8th February 1587, aged 45.

  • Mary was highly educated, she spoke multiple languages, enjoyed riding and hunting and was an accomplished embroiderer and musician.

  • After her execution, the executioner realised Mary's dress was moving, her dog then appeared from under it.

  • Mary's first husband, Francis, died 2 years into the marriage, making Mary a widow at 18.

  • Marys son, James, became the King of England and Scotland after Queen Elizabeth chose him as her successor(she had no children)

  • Mary Queen of Scots became Queen just 6 days after being born, following the death of her father.

  • Mary was betrothed to King Henry VIII's son, Edward at the age of 5 but this was called soon after.

  • Mary's second husband, Lord Darnley, was found murdered. The main suspect would become Mary's 3rd husband.

  • She was held prisoner by her cousin, Elizabeh Tudor, for nearly 20 years in various towns/cities including Sheffield.

  • Mary was abdicated from her Scottish Throne in 1567. Her government had turned against her and her cousin, Elizabeth Tudor, saw her as a threat to the English throne so refused to help
